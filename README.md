# AI-Health-Impact-Dashboard
# Project Title/ Headline: 

As building my first project on AI impact on health dashboard gives me an opportunity to present my view and insights over usage of AI and the related correlation on Health. 

# Overview:
The project AI Impact on Health analyses the healthcare data to understand the impact of AI on health. It includes and generally focuses on age, BMI Category, Risk category, improvement score, the type of AI tool usage and impact on health before or after surging AI. These parameters are recorded on different people along with their participant ID. 

# Objective:
The purpose of this dashboard is help to figure it out the impact of AI over health, to visualise healthcare data and provide insights that can help understand how AI-based health applications relate to health improvement across different user groups.
# Tool Used:

Power BI

Power Query

DAX

Microsoft Excel (Dataset)

# Dashboard Features
Interactive slicers for AI Tool Type, Exercise Level, Age Group, BMI Category, Improvement Category, and Risk Category.

KPI cards displaying Health Improvement, Health Score Before AI, Risk Category, and Age Group.

Analysis of BMI distribution by gender.

AI health app usage by BMI category and age group.

Exercise hours across age groups.

Health score comparison by BMI category.

# Key Questions:
Which age group has the highest risk factor after the surge of AI?

What is the exercise level done by females before AI Usage?

Under what age group count of AI dependency increase?

What is the status of age group under 18-25 regarding AI impact?

# Key Insights
On applying a filter through slicer, the age group under 46-55 who have downloaded mental health apps and is obese got no improvement on their body. The AI  usage only accounts for 6.67%.

Sum of exercise per week is recorded for only 20 hours under the age of 46-55.

As usual females who are suffering from obese  under age group of 55-65 spends 40 hours per week on exercise. 

It is significant to note that 18-25 age group spends 200+ hours per week but less usage of mental health tracker 6.67%.
